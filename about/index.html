~/aboutMe

I am part of a team that creates software products that enable verification of gigascale chip layouts. These verified chip layouts can then be realized in silicon by the foundry using latest (sub-10nm) semiconductor manufacturing technologies. For the youtube folks, here’s a short explanation, describing the problem.

Layout verification is a problem that shares a couple of traits with the currently hot machine learning domain.

  • Both are fairly old problems where computational efficiency is critical for practical applications
  • Just like big-data growth in ML, there has been exponential growth in chip-design data as well, even though the device shrinking may hit a wall beyond 1nm, parallel processors, other design enhancement would keep accelerating the growth in design data as well.

In short, computational efficiency, accuracy, and scalability are of utmost importance in both these areas. I just mentioned the analogy to machine-learning in order to emphasize following point: more often than not, its the second-level problems (below the surface) which can really enable a given technology forward and more often than not, problems like computational efficiency, scalibility manifest in the very small and the very big, and I like to work on such problems.

Prior to software engineering, I worked on chip-design, chip-design automation areas as a graduate student for my Ph.D. project at Electrical Engineering department, University of Minnesota, in the VLSI-EDA group. I studied the performance degradation caused due to `aging’ in metal wires networks that connect transistors (interconnects) in a high-performance semiconductor chip. This involved deep-dives into physics, probability, and computer science to come up with a physical statistical model, which could be efficiently applied to to analyze the direct circuit impact of wire aging induced degradation in modern ICs.

I plan to update this webpage with my projects (big and small) as well as share other interests of mine, in the hope of getting to know folks who are interested in similar areas.
