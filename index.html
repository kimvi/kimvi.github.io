I completed my Ph.D at the department of Electrical Engineering at the University of Minnesota. I worked in the area of computer-aided design for integrated circuits in VLSI-EDA group. I studied performance degradation caused by {aging} of metal wire networks that connect transistors (interconnects) in a high-performance semiconductor chip. As a part of my research, I dabbed into the phyisics (aging mechanism), statistics (agins mechanism are non-deterministic), and efficient numerical computation (efficient analysis of aging in a chip that containts billions of wires). We published our work in top conferences like Design Automation Conference.
